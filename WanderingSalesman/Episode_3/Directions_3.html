My second version worked for slightly larger sizes -- up to 15 or a little larger. But beyond that it took a long time also. To get this higher we need a different kind of change. This time around there's just a single, one-word hint: "memoization".

One other problem to watch out for: once the answers get big enough, most languages won't let you use a normal int anymore -- you will need some sort of bigint implementation.

This step illustrates a fairly general principle of optimization: Caching makes a difference.
